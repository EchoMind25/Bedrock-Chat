## Why You Need Developer Access

Bot creation requires the developer role on your account. This is granted through a short application that an admin reviews.

## Applying for Developer Access

<StepByStep title="Application Steps">

1. **Sign in to Bedrock Chat.** Create an account first if you don't have one.
2. **Open Settings.** Click your profile picture or the gear icon, then navigate to the **Developer** section.
3. **Click "Apply for Developer Access."** This opens the application form.
4. **Complete the form.** Three fields, described below.
5. **Submit.** An admin will review your application.
6. **Wait for approval.** You'll receive developer permissions once approved.

</StepByStep>

## Application Fields

### Email Address

The email where the team can reach you about your bot development. You can use the same email as your Bedrock Chat account.

### Intended Use

A short description (up to 500 characters) of what you plan to build. This doesn't need to be a detailed spec. Examples:

- "A homework reminder bot for my study group."
- "A welcome bot that greets new server members."
- "A trivia game bot for my friend group."

You can build different bots later. This field helps admins understand your general intent.

### Data Processing Agreement (DPA)

You must accept the DPA before submitting.

<WhatThisMeans term="Data Processing Agreement (DPA)">
A **DPA** is a legal agreement governing how you handle user data accessed through your bot. It's standard for any platform that processes user information. By accepting, you commit to not sharing, selling, or misusing any user data your bot encounters.
</WhatThisMeans>

## After You Apply

An admin reviews your application manually. They verify that your intended use is reasonable and that you've accepted the DPA.

Most applications are approved quickly. If more information is needed, the admin will contact you via email.

### What Developer Access Grants

- **Bot registration.** You can create and manage bot applications.
- **Bot analytics.** Usage metrics for your bots (message counts, active users, etc.).

Publishing bots to the public directory requires a separate approval after your first bot passes review.

## Approval Timeline

There's no guaranteed turnaround. Some applications are reviewed in minutes; others may take a day or two depending on admin availability. You only need to apply once — the developer role is permanent.

## Guidelines

**Rate limit on applications.** You can submit up to 3 applications per hour. If rejected, read the feedback, revise your intended use, and resubmit.

**Be specific about your plans.** Vague descriptions are the most common reason for rejection. A sentence or two about your bot's purpose is enough.

**Your plans can change.** The developer role isn't restricted to what you described in the application. You can build any type of bot.

## Troubleshooting

**"I can't find the Developer section in Settings."**
Make sure you're running the latest version of Bedrock Chat. Try refreshing the page. The Developer section is inside your account settings.

**"My application was rejected."**
Read the admin's feedback. The most common cause is a description that's too vague. Add more detail about what your bot will do, then resubmit.

**"I haven't heard back in over a week."**
Reach out in the Bedrock Community server. An admin can check the status of your application.

<PrivacyNote>
Your application data (email and intended use) is visible only to Bedrock Chat admins. It is not shared with third parties or made public.
</PrivacyNote>
