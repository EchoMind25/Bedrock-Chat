## Your Bot Is Live

After approval, your bot appears in the Bedrock Chat bot directory. Server owners can browse the directory and install your bot.

What changes:

- Your bot is **visible** to all Bedrock Chat users.
- Server owners can **install** it on their servers.
- Your bot's profile displays its name, description, and privacy policy.
- Install count is visible in your Developer Portal.

## Tracking Installs

View your install count in the **Developer Portal** by clicking on your bot. This number reflects how many servers currently have your bot installed.

## Updating Your Bot

You can update your bot at any time after approval.

### Updating via the Developer Portal

<StepByStep title="Update via the Developer Portal">

1. Go to the **Developer Portal**.
2. Click on your bot.
3. Modify the fields you want to update.
4. Click **Save Changes**.

</StepByStep>

Some changes apply immediately. Others require re-review:

| Change | Requires Re-review |
|---|---|
| Description | No |
| Avatar/picture | No |
| Privacy policy URL | No (keep it accurate) |
| Bot name | Yes |
| Webhook URL | Yes |
| Scopes/permissions | Yes |
| Teen-safe flag | Yes |

Changes that require re-review set your bot's status to **pending**. Your bot continues to operate with its previous configuration while the review is in progress.

### Updating via the API

Update your bot with a PATCH request:

```javascript
// Replace BOT_ID with your actual bot ID
const updateUrl = "https://bedrock.chat/api/platform/bots/YOUR_BOT_ID";

const response = await fetch(updateUrl, {
  method: "PATCH",
  headers: {
    "Content-Type": "application/json",
    "Authorization": "Bearer your-auth-token"
  },
  body: JSON.stringify({
    description: "My updated bot description."
  })
});

const result = await response.json();
console.log("Bot updated:", result);
```

Include only the fields you want to change.

## Changing Your Webhook URL

Webhook URL changes require verification and re-review.

<StepByStep title="Change Your Webhook URL">

1. Go to the **Developer Portal**.
2. Click on your bot.
3. Enter the new **webhook URL** (must be HTTPS).
4. Click **Verify Webhook** to test the new URL.
5. Click **Save Changes**.
6. Wait for re-review.

</StepByStep>

Your bot continues using the old URL until the new one is approved. Do not disable the old URL until the change is approved.

## Handling Suspension

Approved bots can be suspended for policy violations. Common causes:

- **Content violations.** Bot started producing content that breaks platform rules.
- **User reports.** Multiple users reported the bot for harmful behavior.
- **Extended downtime.** Bot stopped responding for a prolonged period.
- **Security issues.** A vulnerability was discovered.
- **Privacy violations.** Bot collected data it was not approved for.

### Resolving Suspension

<StepByStep title="Resolving a Suspension">

1. Read the suspension notice in your Developer Portal.
2. Identify the root cause.
3. Fix the issue completely.
4. Test the fix.
5. Contact the review team through the Developer Portal.
6. Explain the fix and your plan to prevent recurrence.
7. Wait for re-review.

</StepByStep>

<PrivacyNote>
Privacy violation suspensions may require you to delete improperly collected data. The review team will specify what is needed. Repeated privacy violations can result in permanent removal.
</PrivacyNote>

## Ongoing Responsibilities

### Uptime

Server owners depend on your bot. Minimize downtime. If maintenance is necessary, keep it brief.

### Privacy Policy Maintenance

Update your privacy policy whenever your bot's data practices change. The policy must always reflect current behavior.

### Issue Response

Address user reports promptly. Bots with unresolved issues may be suspended.

### Rate Limits

Stay within platform rate limits:

- **1 message per second** per webhook
- **15 webhooks maximum** per server

Contact the Bedrock Chat team if you need higher limits.

### Content Guidelines

Content rules apply permanently, not just during review:

- No hate speech
- No harassment
- No NSFW content in teen-safe servers
- No spam
- No illegal content

### Error Monitoring

Check your bot's logs regularly. Fix errors before they are reported by users.

## Removing Your Bot

To remove your bot from the directory, go to the Developer Portal, click on your bot, and select **Remove from Directory**.

This action:

- Removes your bot from the directory
- Uninstalls it from all servers
- Stops all webhook messages

Removal is permanent. Returning to the directory requires a new submission and review.
