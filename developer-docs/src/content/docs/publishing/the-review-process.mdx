## Status Lifecycle

Your bot moves through these statuses during and after review:

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Pending** | In the queue. No reviewer has been assigned yet. |
| **Under Review** | A reviewer is actively evaluating your bot. |
| **Approved** | Your bot passed review and is live. |
| **Rejected** | Your bot did not pass. Review notes explain what to fix. |
| **Suspended** | Your bot was live but was taken down due to a violation. |

Most bots move from **pending** to **approved** or **rejected**. The **suspended** status only applies to previously approved bots.

## Review Timeline

Review times depend on queue volume:

- **Most bots:** 1 to 3 business days.
- **Simple bots** (webhook-only): often faster.
- **Complex bots:** may take longer.

You receive a notification when the review is complete. No need to check manually.

## What Reviewers Evaluate

A human reviewer evaluates your bot. Here is what they check.

### 1. Functionality

The reviewer tests your bot to confirm:

- It responds to commands.
- Responses arrive within 5 seconds.
- Behavior matches the description.

### 2. Description Accuracy

Your bot's name and description must match its actual functionality. A bot named "Weather Bot" that plays trivia will be rejected.

### 3. Privacy Policy

The reviewer reads your privacy policy and checks that:

- The URL loads a real page.
- The policy describes what data your bot accesses.
- The policy matches your bot's actual behavior.

<PrivacyNote>
Your privacy policy is one of the most common reasons for rejection. Make it accurate and specific.
</PrivacyNote>

### 4. Error Handling

The reviewer will attempt to trigger errors by sending:

- Unexpected commands
- Very long messages
- Empty messages
- Special characters

Your bot should handle all of these without crashing or exposing raw errors.

### 5. Content Guidelines

The reviewer checks compliance with content rules:

- No hate speech
- No harassment
- No NSFW content in teen-safe bots
- No spam behavior
- No illegal content

### 6. Security

The reviewer verifies:

- Webhook URL uses HTTPS
- Error messages do not expose secrets
- Bot does not ask users for passwords or tokens

### 7. Teen Safety

Bots marked as teen-safe receive additional review. The reviewer confirms that no path through your bot's functionality can produce inappropriate content for young users.

<PrivacyNote>
Bots marked as teen-safe get additional scrutiny. Any content safety issue results in rejection.
</PrivacyNote>

## Handling Rejection

Rejection is not final. Many bots are rejected on the first submission.

When rejected, you receive:

- A **reason** explaining the issue.
- **Review notes** with specific items to fix (up to 1,000 characters).
- Instructions for **resubmission**.

### Resubmission Process

1. Read the review notes carefully.
2. Fix every issue mentioned.
3. Test your bot again.
4. Update your bot's details in the Developer Portal if needed.
5. Click **Submit for Review** again.

There is no limit on resubmissions.

## Handling Suspension

Suspension applies to bots that were previously approved. A bot can be suspended for:

- Content guideline violations
- User reports of harmful behavior
- Extended downtime or unresponsiveness
- Security vulnerabilities
- Inaccurate privacy policy

### What Happens on Suspension

- The bot stops working immediately.
- New installations are blocked.
- Existing installations stop receiving messages.
- You receive a notification with the reason.

### Resolving Suspension

1. Read the suspension reason in the Developer Portal.
2. Fix the underlying issue.
3. Contact the review team through the Developer Portal.
4. Explain what was fixed and how you will prevent recurrence.
5. Wait for re-review.

Repeated suspensions may result in developer account review.

## Review Tips

To improve your chances of first-time approval:

- **Test every command** before submitting.
- **Write a clear description** that matches your bot's behavior.
- **Customize your privacy policy.** Do not submit an unedited template.
- **Handle errors gracefully.** No raw errors in user-facing messages.
- **Keep your bot online** during the review period.
- **Set the teen-safe flag accurately.** Do not mark as teen-safe if it is not.

## Review Notes

When a reviewer finishes, they write notes explaining their decision. Notes can be up to **1,000 characters** and are visible in your Developer Portal. They list specific issues on rejection and may include improvement suggestions on approval.
