## Age Requirements

Bedrock Chat is used by families and teenagers. Some users are under 13. All bots must comply with child safety regulations regardless of their intended audience.

<WhatThisMeans term="COPPA">
**COPPA** (Children's Online Privacy Protection Act) is a United States law that requires websites and apps to protect the personal information of children under 13. It restricts data collection from minors and requires verifiable parental consent.
</WhatThisMeans>

<PrivacyNote>
Bedrock Chat is **COPPA compliant**. All bots on the platform must maintain this compliance.
</PrivacyNote>

## Core Rule

Your bot must never collect, store, or share personal information -- data that can identify a real person, such as real names, home addresses, schools, phone numbers, email addresses, photos of faces, or locations -- from any user.

If your bot does not collect personal information from anyone, it cannot accidentally collect it from a child. This is the safest and simplest approach.

## Prohibited Behaviors

### Requesting Personal Details

Your bot must not ask users for their real name, location, school, or any other identifying information.

<PrivacyNote>
Even if a user volunteers personal information in a message, your bot should **not store it**. Discard it and do not repeat it back.
</PrivacyNote>

### Age-Based Differentiation

Your bot must not ask for a user's age, track age data, or deliver different content based on age. Having a "kid-friendly mode" or "adult mode" implies age tracking, which constitutes collecting personal information.

<PrivacyNote>
Do not check whether a user is under 13 or under 18. Build your bot to be safe for **all ages** by default.
</PrivacyNote>

### Unsolicited Direct Messages to Minors

Your bot must not send direct messages to users under 18 unless they message your bot first. Even then, keep responses brief and relevant.

### Storing Data Without Disclosure

If your bot stores any data (even a username for a leaderboard), you must disclose this in your bot submission. Specify what you store, where it is stored, and why. Submissions without this disclosure are rejected.

### Sharing Data With External Services

Your bot must not send user data to external websites or services. When your bot calls an external API (such as a weather service), send only the request parameters, never user information.

<PrivacyNote>
When calling external services, send only the query (e.g., "weather in Paris"), never user identifiers or metadata (e.g., "User JohnDoe wants weather").
</PrivacyNote>

## The isTeenSafe Flag

The `isTeenSafe` flag on your bot registration indicates your bot is safe for users under 18. When you enable this flag, you are certifying that:

- Your bot does not collect personal information
- Your bot does not display content intended only for adults
- Your bot does not use violent or inappropriate language
- Your bot does not encourage users to share personal details
- Your bot does not link to websites with adult content

### Review Process for isTeenSafe Bots

Bots with `isTeenSafe` enabled receive additional scrutiny during review. Reviewers will attempt to elicit personal information requests, verify consistent behavior across users, and audit all possible bot responses.

<PrivacyNote>
If your bot is marked `isTeenSafe` and reviewers find compliance issues, the bot will be **rejected**. You can fix the issues and resubmit.
</PrivacyNote>

## Bots Without isTeenSafe

Even if your bot is not marked as teen-safe, follow these rules. You cannot control which servers install your bot, and those servers may have teenage members.

Build every bot as if young users will interact with it.

## Pre-Submission Checklist

<StepByStep title="Safety Checklist">

1. **No personal data collection.** Your bot never asks for real names, ages, locations, schools, or contact information.

2. **No age tracking.** Your bot treats every user the same regardless of age.

3. **No unsolicited DMs.** Your bot does not message users who have not messaged it first.

4. **No adult content.** Your bot does not send, link to, or reference inappropriate content.

5. **No external data sharing.** Your bot does not send user information to outside services.

6. **Data storage disclosed.** If your bot stores anything, you have documented what, where, and why in your submission.

7. **isTeenSafe flag set correctly.** If your bot meets all the above criteria, enable the flag.

</StepByStep>

## Enforcement

Bots that violate child safety rules are removed from the platform. Serious violations have no warning period.

Collecting personal information from a child may constitute a legal violation. Bedrock Chat will cooperate with authorities when required.

<PrivacyNote>
Child safety compliance is a **platform requirement**. Bots that do not meet these standards cannot be published.
</PrivacyNote>

## Getting Help

If you are unsure whether your bot meets these requirements, contact the review team before submitting. It is faster to resolve questions upfront than to go through a rejection cycle.
