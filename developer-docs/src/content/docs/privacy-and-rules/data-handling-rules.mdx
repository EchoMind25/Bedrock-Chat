## Data Processing Requirements

If your bot interacts with user data in any way, these rules apply. Most originate from privacy regulations and are enforced at the platform level.

## Data Processing Agreement

Before your bot can go live, you must accept the Data Processing Agreement (DPA). This happens during bot registration.

<WhatThisMeans term="DPA">
A **Data Processing Agreement** (DPA) is a legally binding agreement between you and Bedrock Chat. It defines your obligations for handling user data responsibly. DPAs are required by privacy laws in many countries.
</WhatThisMeans>

### DPA Summary

**Your obligations:**
- Use data only for your bot's stated purpose
- Never sell, share, or distribute user data
- Delete data when it is no longer needed
- Report data incidents to Bedrock Chat

**Platform obligations:**
- Provide your bot only the data it has permission to access
- Protect data on platform servers
- Notify you of privacy rule changes
- Assist with legal compliance

<PrivacyNote>
The DPA is stored with a **timestamp and version number**. If the DPA is updated, you must accept the new version before your bot can continue operating.
</PrivacyNote>

## Account Deletion Handling

When a user deletes their Bedrock Chat account, the platform handles cleanup automatically.

### What the Platform Does

Messages remain, but the author's user ID is removed. The message content persists; the author becomes anonymous.

<WhatThisMeans term="cascading deletion">
**Cascading deletion** means that when one record is deleted, related records are cleaned up automatically. When a user account is deleted, their user ID is removed from all associated messages.
</WhatThisMeans>

### What Your Bot Must Handle

If your bot stored a user ID, that ID will no longer resolve to a user. Your bot should handle this gracefully without errors.

If your bot stores data outside of Bedrock Chat (in your own database), you are responsible for deleting that user's data when notified.

<PrivacyNote>
Bedrock Chat handles platform-side cleanup automatically. If your bot maintains external data stores, **you are responsible** for deletion compliance.
</PrivacyNote>

## Right to Erasure

Users have the legal right to request deletion of their personal data under GDPR (Europe) and CCPA (California).

<WhatThisMeans term="GDPR">
**GDPR** (General Data Protection Regulation) is a European privacy law that gives people control over their personal data, including the right to have it deleted on request.
</WhatThisMeans>

### Deletion Process

<StepByStep title="When a User Requests Data Deletion">

1. **User requests account deletion** through their Bedrock Chat settings.

2. **Platform removes their data** from internal systems. User IDs are cleared from messages and account data is deleted.

3. **Your bot receives a webhook notification** (if it stores data externally) indicating the user was deleted.

4. **You delete that user's data** from your external systems within 30 days.

</StepByStep>

<PrivacyNote>
If your bot does not store data outside of Bedrock Chat, you do not need to take any action. The platform handles everything.
</PrivacyNote>

## Right to Access

Users can request a copy of all personal data held about them. Bedrock Chat provides a JSON data export covering platform data.

If your bot stores data separately, users may request their data from you directly. Keep your data organized so you can fulfill these requests.

## Data Retention

Keep data only as long as your bot actively needs it.

**Active use:** A trivia bot keeping the current game score is acceptable.

**Task complete:** When the game ends or the task finishes, delete the data.

**When in doubt:** Retain data for no more than 30 days, then delete it.

<PrivacyNote>
The less data you retain, the lower your compliance burden. If your bot does not need to remember something, do not store it.
</PrivacyNote>

### Data You Must Never Store

Some data must never be stored by your bot, even temporarily:

- Real names, addresses, or school names
- Email addresses or phone numbers
- Dates of birth or ages
- Photos of real people
- Location information
- Any data from the [restricted access list](/privacy-and-rules/what-your-bot-cannot-do)

If your bot receives personal information in a message, discard it. Do not store or echo it back.

## CCPA Compliance

If California residents use your bot, additional requirements apply.

<WhatThisMeans term="CCPA">
**CCPA** (California Consumer Privacy Act) gives California residents the right to know what data is collected about them and to opt out of data sales.
</WhatThisMeans>

**No data sales.** Your bot must never sell user data.

**Data disclosure.** If a California user asks what data you have about them, you must respond within 45 days.

**Opt-out.** If a user asks you to stop collecting their data, you must comply. Your bot must still function without collecting data.

<PrivacyNote>
If your bot does not collect or store personal data, CCPA compliance is straightforward: inform the user that no personal data is collected.
</PrivacyNote>

## Privacy Policy

If your bot stores any data, you must provide a privacy policy. It does not need to be written by a lawyer. It needs to be accurate and clear.

### Required Contents

<StepByStep title="Writing Your Privacy Policy">

1. **What data your bot collects.** Be specific. "We store your username and game score" is correct. "We may collect data" is too vague.

2. **Why your bot collects it.** State the purpose. "We store your score so you can continue your game later."

3. **Where the data is stored.** Name the service. "Scores are stored in a Supabase database."

4. **How long you keep it.** Be specific. "Scores are deleted 30 days after your last game."

5. **How users can delete their data.** Provide a mechanism. "Type `/delete-my-data` to remove your scores."

6. **Contact information.** Provide a way to reach you. An email address or Bedrock Chat username works.

</StepByStep>

Include your privacy policy in your bot submission. The review team evaluates it as part of the approval process.

## Quick Reference

| Rule | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Accept the DPA | Required during registration |
| Handle account deletion | Do not error when a user ID no longer resolves |
| Delete external data on request | Within 30 days of notification |
| Provide data on request | Respond to user data requests |
| Retain data only as needed | Delete when the task is complete |
| Never sell data | No exceptions |
| Provide a privacy policy | Accurate, specific, and accessible |
